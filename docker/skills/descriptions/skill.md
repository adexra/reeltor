# Caption Writing Skill — Source of Truth

This file defines the rules for every Instagram caption generated by this system.
All captions must pass every rule below before being shown to the user.

---

## Structure

An Instagram caption has three zones:

**Zone 1 — First line (the hook continuation)**
The only text visible before "more" is tapped. Must create tension, curiosity, or strong emotion. The viewer just read the reel hook — this line pays it off and makes them want to read further.

Never open with:
- "I" ("I've been thinking…", "I used to…")
- The brand name
- A question starting with "Are you", "Have you", "Do you"
- A numbered list ("1. Start by…")

**Zone 2 — Body**
Delivers value, story, or proof. Short paragraphs only — 1 to 2 sentences per block. White space between blocks. No walls of text.

Banned formatting:
- Numbered lists (1. 2. 3.)
- Bullet points (• -)
- Bold markdown (**text**)
- Instagram renders none of these — they look broken

**Zone 3 — CTA (Call to Action)**
Last line. Standalone. Specific. Low-friction.

Good CTAs:
- "Save this before your next trip"
- "Drop a emoji if this is you"
- "DM me 'READY' and I'll send the full list"
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"

Bad CTAs:
- "Follow for more"
- "Like and share"
- "Check the link in bio" (too vague)
- No CTA at all

---

## Voice

Write like a person talking to one other person. Not a brand. Not a coach. Not a productivity account.

Test: read the caption out loud. Does it sound like something a real person would say? Or does it sound like a press release?

---

## Emojis

Maximum 2 per caption total. Used for rhythm or emphasis only. Never as decoration between every sentence.

Good: one emoji in the CTA line to make the action concrete
Bad: scattered throughout every sentence

---

## Banned phrases

Never use these — they signal generic AI content instantly:

- "game-changer"
- "unlock"
- "transform your"
- "dive into"
- "in today's world"
- "the truth is"
- "let's be honest"
- "here's the thing"
- "it's no secret"
- "you need to know"
- "this is why"
- "don't miss out"
- "level up"

---

## Caption formats

Five structural formats — use a different one for each of the 5 options:

**Format A — Single punch**
One tight paragraph, 3-4 sentences. Dense but readable. CTA at end.
Best for: bold claims, strong opinions.

**Format B — Two-beat**
First paragraph creates tension or raises the problem.
Second paragraph resolves it and ends with CTA.
Best for: before/after, problem/solution.

**Format C — Bold open**
First line: a sharp, bold, standalone statement (not a question).
Second line: one supporting sentence.
Third line: CTA.
Best for: counterintuitive takes, strong positioning.

**Format D — Mid-story start**
Drops the reader into the middle of a scene.
Short narrative, 3-5 sentences. Ends with CTA.
Best for: travel, lifestyle, relatable chaos.

**Format E — Three beats**
Three short standalone lines, each a complete thought.
Last line is the CTA.
Best for: insights written as prose lines, not bullet points.

---

## Validation checklist

Every caption must pass all of these before it is shown to the user:

- First line does NOT start with "I", the brand name, or a weak question opener
- No numbered lists, bullet points, or bold markdown
- No banned phrases
- Body is short paragraphs with line breaks
- CTA is present, specific, and in the last standalone line
- Maximum 2 emojis total
- Reads like a human talking to one person
- Tone matches the specified tone
- Written in the language of the target audience
